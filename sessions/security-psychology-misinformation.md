# The Intersectionality of Human Psychology, Security and The Era of AI and Misinformation

https://www.socallinuxexpo.org/scale/23x/presentations/intersectionality-human-psychology-security-and-era-ai-and-misinformation

- **Date**: Saturday, March 7, 2026
- **Time**: 12:30 - 13:30
- **Location**: Room 101
- **Speaker(s)**: Autumn Nash, Software Engineer, Azure Linux, Microsoft

> The presentation examines how cybercriminals exploit human vulnerabilities through social engineering and psychological manipulation. Cybercrime causes significant economic damage, with predictions of "$6 trillion USD globally in 2021." Companies face approximately 21–24 days of downtime following ransomware incidents. Technology advances faster than public education and awareness can keep pace, and younger demographics (under 35) obtain news primarily from TikTok and social media platforms. Cybercriminals leverage psychological tactics—fear and urgency—to manipulate victims into clicking malicious links or divulging credentials. The intersection of psychology and cybersecurity is essential for developing effective defenses and educational initiatives.

## Overview

An exploration of how psychological manipulation and social engineering intersect with modern cybersecurity threats, and why closing the education gap is as important as technical defenses in the age of AI-powered misinformation.
