# Try To Teach a Goldfish to Bark

https://www.socallinuxexpo.org/scale/23x/presentations/try-teach-goldfish-bark

- **Date**: Sunday, March 8, 2026
- **Time**: 12:00 - 13:30
- **Location**: Room 104
- **Speaker(s)**: Khewna Dawar, Executive Coach, TGL Advisory

> The presentation examines organizational misalignment in technical environments. The speaker uses a metaphor about expecting a goldfish to bark—highlighting how hiring criteria often shift once companies scale. Engineering talent selected for problem-solving abilities later faces evaluation on visibility and narrative skills.
>
> Using Industrial-Organizational Psychology, the session explores how these system misalignments drive burnout and erode confidence. "High performers, in particular, internalize this disconnect. System design flaws turn into self-criticism." The talk addresses how impressions form in decision-making contexts and why increased effort sometimes reduces visibility for skilled professionals.
>
> Participants receive tools to diagnose system misalignments, recognize when inner-critic narratives stem from organizational design rather than personal inadequacy, translate technical work into impact-focused outcomes, and make meaningful impressions without performative self-promotion.

## Overview

An application of Industrial-Organizational Psychology to the systemic misalignments in tech organizations that leave high performers burned out and overlooked—and a toolkit for diagnosing and escaping these traps.
